# AI Governance Public Example

This example is synthetic.

It exists to show a narrow AI governance corridor that stays honest about what is documentary, what is machine-checkable, and what remains outside scope.

## Corridor

- intended use and deployment context documentation,
- AI risk management process documentation,
- human oversight role assignment,
- post-deployment monitoring and incident escalation ownership,
- machine-checkable AI-generated content disclosure,
- machine-checkable AI content provenance metadata,
- AI evaluation scope and findings documentation,
- and AI data-quality and lineage governance documentation.

## Intended use

- show how an honest AI governance corridor can mix documentary attestations with a narrow machine-checkable transparency slice,
- give teams a public-safe starting point for EU and NIST AI governance discussions,
- and prove that a blocked corridor can still stay narrow, scoped, and explicit about what it does not certify.
